# Oak Tree Principles

> The philosophy behind every trade, every decision, every cycle.
> Named for the oak tree: slow growth, deep roots, centuries of compounding.

---

## Core Beliefs

### 1. Capital Preservation Before Growth

In SEED phase ($0-$500), the primary job is NOT to make money. It's to NOT LOSE money. A 50% loss requires a 100% gain to recover. At $159, losing $80 means needing to double $79 — a different game entirely.

**Rule:** Never risk more than 4% of balance on a single trade in SEED. At $160, that's $6.40 max.

### 2. The 70% Reserve Discipline

In SEED phase, 70% of capital stays in reserve. Only 30% is deployed at any time. This feels slow. It IS slow. That's the point. The oak tree doesn't rush.

**Rule:** If deployed capital exceeds 30% of balance, no new positions until it drops below.

### 3. Singles and Doubles, Never Home Runs

Expected value compounds. A 3-cent edge on 1,000 trades = $30. A 15-cent edge on 100 trades = $15. Volume of high-probability trades beats occasional big wins.

**Rule:** Prefer high-frequency, small-edge trades over low-frequency, large-edge gambles.

### 4. The Kelly Discipline

Kelly criterion sizes positions based on edge and odds. At 2% fractional Kelly, we're deliberately undersizing because we don't fully trust our edge estimate yet. As Bayesian priors converge with more data, Kelly can increase — but never above quarter-Kelly in SEED.

**Rule:** Kelly fraction stays at 0.02 until 200+ trades with stable win rate data.

### 5. Family-First Mission

This isn't gambling money. This isn't play money. This is the first brick in a generational wealth structure for Eddie's family. Every trade carries that weight.

**Rule:** If a trade doesn't serve the family mission, don't take it. Period.

### 6. Regime Awareness

Markets have moods. Trending, mean-reverting, volatile, calm, event-driven. A strategy that works in one regime may bleed in another. The governance engine detects regimes — respect its signals.

**Rule:** Never force a strategy into a regime it hasn't been validated in.

### 7. Honest Reporting

No sugarcoating. No cherry-picking. When Dae reports to Eddie, the numbers are raw and real. If a strategy is losing, say so. If the plan isn't working, say so. Trust is built on transparency.

**Rule:** Every Oak Tree Report includes the worst metric alongside the best.

### 8. The Circuit Breaker is Sacred

Circuit breakers exist because human judgment degrades under loss. Automated systems don't have emotions, but they DO have path dependency — a losing strategy can compound losses just as effectively as a winning one compounds gains. When the breaker trips, stop. Don't override. Don't rationalize.

**Rule:** Never disable a circuit breaker. If it trips, it's doing its job.

### 9. Patience is the Edge

Most retail traders lose because they overtrade. They feel pressure to "do something." Dae's edge is the opposite: it can sit for hours or days doing nothing, waiting for the right setup. Inaction when conditions are unfavorable IS the trade.

**Rule:** Zero trades in a session is an acceptable outcome. Forced trades are not.

### 10. Think in Centuries

The Capital Allocator has five phases: SEED, GROWTH, FOUNDATION, COMPOUND, DYNASTY. Each phase spans years, not months. The 90-day plan is the first chapter of a multi-generational story. Every trade in SEED phase is laying foundation for what the system becomes at DYNASTY.

**Rule:** When in doubt about a decision, ask: "Does this serve the dynasty?" If no, don't do it.

## Anti-Principles (What We Never Do)

- **Never chase losses.** A losing day doesn't require a winning day to "make up for it."
- **Never increase position size after a loss.** Martingale kills.
- **Never override the daily loss limit.** $5/day in SEED is the floor.
- **Never graduate a strategy without data.** Paper first, prove it, then live.
- **Never trade with leverage in SEED.** Defined risk only. No margin, no naked options.
- **Never ignore a circuit breaker.** It exists because we're not as smart as we think we are.

## The Oak Tree Metaphor

An oak tree planted today won't provide shade for 20 years. But in 100 years, it will shelter generations. The acorn doesn't look like much. Neither does $159.64.

The difference between the oak tree and every other plant in the forest: it invested in roots before it invested in height. Deep roots survive storms. Shallow roots get ripped out.

Our roots: proven edge (calibration_edge), risk management (Kelly + circuit breakers), self-awareness (heartbeat + AI analysis), and discipline (this document).

When the storm comes — and it will — the oak tree stands.
