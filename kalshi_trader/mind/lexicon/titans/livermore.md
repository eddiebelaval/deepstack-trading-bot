# Jesse Livermore — The Tape Reader, The Patient Predator

## Core Principles

- **The big money is in the waiting:** Livermore's greatest insight: most traders lose money by trading. The market rewards patience and punishes activity. Sit tight until the setup is undeniable.
- **Pyramiding into winners:** Never add to a losing position. Add to winners that are confirming your thesis. Your first position is a probe — your second is conviction — your third is the kill.
- **The market is never wrong, opinions are:** Don't argue with price. If the tape says you're wrong, you're wrong. Your thesis doesn't matter if the market disagrees.
- **Leading stocks lead:** The strongest names in a move tell you the character of the move. Watch what's leading, not what's lagging.
- **Pivotal points:** Markets pivot at specific price levels where the balance of power shifts. These are not support/resistance — they are moments where the market's character changes.
- **Money management is everything:** Livermore went bankrupt multiple times because he violated his own sizing rules. He'd have been the richest man in history if he'd followed them.

## Mental Models

- **The Line of Least Resistance:** Every market has a direction of least resistance. Don't fight it. Trade with it or sit out. The direction is revealed by the tape — price action, volume, the behavior of the market when it should move but doesn't.
- **Time element:** "There is a time to go long, a time to go short, and a time to go fishing." The fishing part is the hardest and most profitable. Most losses come from trading when you should be fishing.
- **Danger signals:** When a stock fails to do what it should after a confirming setup — THAT is the danger signal. Not the loss, but the failure to confirm. Lack of confirmation is louder than any decline.
- **The trend within the trend:** Markets move in waves within waves. The day trend, the intermediate trend, the long trend. Know which one you're trading. Mixing timeframes is ruin.
- **Selling into strength, buying into weakness:** The crowd buys breakouts and sells breakdowns. Livermore bought into weakness (when the direction of least resistance was up) and sold into strength (when the crowd was euphoric).

## Prediction Market Translation

- **Sitting tight on calibration_edge:** The strategy that works is the one you don't fiddle with. Livermore's biggest regret was selling too soon when the thesis was still intact. Dae's calibration_edge at 87% win rate IS the big position — don't rotate away from it because other strategies look exciting.
- **Pivotal points in prediction markets:** When a contract's price stalls at a key level (30c, 50c, 70c) despite new information, THAT is the pivotal point. The next move from that level reveals the market's true direction.
- **The tape of Kalshi:** Volume patterns, price velocity, order flow — these are the modern tape. When volume surges but price doesn't move, someone large is distributing. When price moves on low volume, it's a false move.
- **Adding to winners:** If a contract moves 5c in your direction after entry, the thesis is confirming. Add. If it moves against you immediately, the thesis is failing. Cut.
- **Fishing cycles:** When Dae's governance detects regime transitions (confidence < 0.4), THAT IS FISHING TIME. Don't trade. Wait for the new regime to establish. Livermore's biggest wins came after his longest periods of inactivity.

## When This Applies

- **Regime alignment:** TRENDING_UP/DOWN (trend is the line of least resistance), LOW_VOL_CALM (patience rewarded — wait for the setup, don't force trades)
- **Signal:** Strong directional moves with confirming volume, contract price stalling at pivotal levels, regime recently established with rising confidence
- **Anti-signal:** HIGH_VOL_CHOPPY (no clear line of least resistance), MEAN_REVERTING (Livermore was a trend follower at heart — mean reversion is not his game)

## Capital Phase Alignment

- **SEED:** Livermore's core lesson — protect capital above all else. He went bankrupt by sizing too big too early. In SEED, trade small, wait for the fat pitch.
- **GROWTH:** Start pyramiding. Use initial wins as conviction to add to winners.
- **DYNASTY:** "It was never my thinking that made the big money. It was always my sitting." At dynasty scale, the patience IS the strategy.
