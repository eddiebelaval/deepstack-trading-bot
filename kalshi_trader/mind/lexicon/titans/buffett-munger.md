# Buffett & Munger — Patient Capital, Relentless Discipline

## Core Principles

- **Margin of safety:** Never pay fair value. Buy at a discount to intrinsic worth so you survive being wrong. If a Kalshi contract has a true probability of 80% and it's priced at 75c, the margin of safety is only 5c. If it's priced at 65c, the margin is 15c. Buffett would only take the second trade.
- **Circle of competence:** Only trade what you understand deeply. Ignorance dressed as confidence is the fastest way to lose. For Dae: only deploy strategies whose edge mechanism is explainable in one sentence. If you can't explain WHY it works, you don't understand it.
- **Mr. Market is manic-depressive:** The market offers prices, not wisdom. Let it serve you, not instruct you. When Mr. Market panics and prices a high-probability contract at 50c instead of 75c, that's a gift. When Mr. Market is euphoric and prices a 50/50 contract at 80c, that's a trap.
- **Moats over momentum:** Durable edges compound. Temporary edges revert. Calibration_edge has a structural moat — favorite-longshot bias is a human behavioral pattern that doesn't go away. Momentum trading has no moat — any pattern that works gets arbitraged.
- **Mental models (Munger):** Invert, always invert. Think about what would guarantee failure and avoid it. Before taking a trade: "What would make this a guaranteed loser?" If you can't identify the failure mode, you don't understand the trade.
- **Time is the friend of the wonderful business:** Patience is not passive — it is the highest-conviction position. Holding cash while waiting for a mispriced contract is not "doing nothing" — it's rejecting unfavorable terms.

## Mental Models

- **Inversion (Munger's Superpower):** Don't ask "how do I make money?" Ask "how do I guarantee LOSING money?" and avoid those things.
  - Trading without edge → guaranteed loss (fees eat you)
  - Oversizing positions → guaranteed ruin (Kelly mathematics)
  - Adding to losers → guaranteed deepening losses
  - Fighting the regime → guaranteed inefficiency
  - Ignoring transaction costs → guaranteed edge erosion
  The path to wealth is not adding brilliance. It's subtracting stupidity.

- **The 20-Slot Punch Card:** Buffett said: imagine you have a punch card with 20 slots. Each time you make an investment, you punch a slot. After 20 punches, you can never invest again. The discipline this creates: only take the BEST trades. The ones with the widest margin of safety, the clearest edge, the most asymmetric risk/reward. Dae's version: what if you could only take 20 trades this month? Which ones would make the cut?

- **Temperature-Taking:** Munger evaluates an opportunity in 30 seconds — "Is this one of the best 2-3 ideas I've ever seen? If not, pass." Dae's EV gate does this systematically: if expected_value_net_cents <= 0, reject. But Buffett-Munger thinking goes further: even positive EV trades get rejected if they're not in the top 10% of EV.

- **The Newspaper Test:** Would you be comfortable seeing this trade reported in the newspaper tomorrow? If a trade requires hiding from scrutiny, it's probably wrong. For Dae: would the Captain's Log entry for this trade make Eddie proud or worried?

- **Opportunity Cost:** Every dollar deployed in Trade A cannot be deployed in Trade B. Buffett doesn't compare a trade to zero — he compares it to the BEST alternative use of that capital. Dae should compare each trade to holding cash + waiting for a better setup.

## Prediction Market Translation

- **Calibration edge IS Buffett:** Favorite-longshot bias is the prediction market equivalent of buying wonderful companies at fair prices. The market systematically overprices longshots and underprices favorites. Buffett would recognize this as "be greedy when others are fearful" applied to probability mispricing.
- **Wait for fat pitches:** Most Kalshi contracts are noise — priced at or near fair value, where the edge is too thin to overcome fees. Buffett would scan 100 contracts and trade 2. Dae's scan-to-trade ratio should be high. A day with zero trades is not a failure — it's discipline.
- **Let time decay work for you:** High-probability contracts that settle at 100 are compounding machines. Each day that passes with the outcome unchanged is profit accruing. Buffett would say: "time is the friend of the wonderful trade."
- **Ignore the crowd:** When everyone piles into a political or weather market, the edge evaporates. The crowd creates a feedback loop that drives prices to consensus — which is where edge goes to die. Wait for the crowd to leave.
- **Inversion for trade selection:** Before each trade, Dae should run the Munger inversion: "What would make this trade a guaranteed loser?" If the answer includes "if the regime shifts" and the regime is unstable (low confidence), pass. If the answer includes "if volume spikes" and volume is spiking, pass.

## When This Applies

- **Regime alignment:** LOW_VOL_CALM (patience rewarded — wait for fat pitches), MEAN_REVERTING (buy the dip — contracts below fair value will revert)
- **Signal:** VIX-equivalent fear spikes, contracts trading 15c+ below fundamental probability, market dislocations from news overreaction, high EV per trade opportunities
- **Anti-signal:** TRENDING markets (Buffett underperforms in momentum runs — his edge is patience, not speed), HIGH_VOL_CHOPPY (margin of safety gets tested too hard too fast)

## Capital Phase Alignment

- **SEED:** Buffett's Rule #1 is everything at SEED. "Never lose money." At $159, a single bad trade costs 3-6% of capital. Only take trades where the margin of safety is enormous. Buffett would run 1-2 strategies max in SEED.
- **GROWTH:** The 20-slot punch card applies. With more capital, the temptation is to trade more. Resist. Quality over quantity. Each trade should still clear the Munger temperature check.
- **FOUNDATION:** Opportunity cost thinking becomes critical. With $5K-$50K, capital allocation IS the strategy. Buffett would concentrate in the 2-3 highest-conviction positions, not spread across 10.
- **COMPOUND:** "Our favorite holding period is forever." At compound scale, the strategies that work should run with minimal interference. Compounding rewards patience and punishes activity.
- **DYNASTY:** Buffett at 93 still holds positions he bought in the 1970s. Dynasty is about generational compounding. The edge isn't the trade — it's the TIME the capital has been compounding. Don't interrupt it.
