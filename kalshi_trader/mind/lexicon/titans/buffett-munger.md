# Buffett & Munger — Patient Capital, Relentless Discipline

## Core Principles

- **Margin of safety:** Never pay fair value. Buy at a discount to intrinsic worth so you survive being wrong.
- **Circle of competence:** Only trade what you understand deeply. Ignorance dressed as confidence is the fastest way to lose.
- **Mr. Market is manic-depressive:** The market offers prices, not wisdom. Let it serve you, not instruct you.
- **Moats over momentum:** Durable edges compound. Temporary edges revert.
- **Mental models (Munger):** Invert, always invert. Think about what would guarantee failure and avoid it.
- **Time is the friend of the wonderful business:** Patience is not passive — it is the highest-conviction position.

## Key Strategies

- Buy when others are fearful — extreme fear creates mispricing
- Concentrate in highest-conviction positions (Buffett runs ~40% in top 5 holdings)
- Hold through volatility if thesis is intact — do not let drawdowns shake you out
- Avoid leverage on core positions — survive first, profit second
- Say no to 99% of opportunities — quality over quantity

## Prediction Market Translation

- **Calibration edge alignment:** Favorite-longshot bias IS the Buffett play — the market systematically overprices longshots and underprices favorites. Buy high-probability contracts at discount.
- **Wait for fat pitches:** Most Kalshi contracts are noise. Only trade when edge is clear and measurable.
- **Let time decay work for you:** High-probability contracts that settle at 100 are compounding machines.
- **Ignore the crowd:** When everyone piles into a political or weather market, the edge evaporates. Wait for the crowd to leave.

## When This Applies

- **Regime alignment:** LOW_VOL_CALM (patience rewarded), MEAN_REVERTING (buy the dip)
- **Signal:** VIX-equivalent fear spikes, contracts trading below fundamental probability, market dislocations from news overreaction
- **Anti-signal:** TRENDING markets (Buffett underperforms in momentum runs), HIGH_VOL_CHOPPY (margin of safety gets tested too hard)

## Key Quotes

- "Be fearful when others are greedy, and greedy when others are fearful."
- "Rule No. 1: Never lose money. Rule No. 2: Never forget Rule No. 1."
- "It's far better to buy a wonderful company at a fair price than a fair company at a wonderful price."
