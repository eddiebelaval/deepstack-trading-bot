# Stanley Druckenmiller — Asymmetric Aggression, Capital Preservation First

## Core Principles

- **Asymmetric risk/reward is the only game worth playing:** Never take a 1:1 bet. Seek setups where the downside is defined and small but the upside is 3x, 5x, 10x. If you can't define the asymmetry, you don't have a trade.
- **Preservation of capital is the foundation:** Druckenmiller made 30% annual returns for 30 years with zero down years. Not by being right more often — by cutting losers ruthlessly and letting winners run. Capital preservation enables aggression.
- **Go for the jugular:** When the setup is right — massive asymmetry, thesis confirmed, market dislocated — bet BIG. Not reckless big. Convicted big. Druckenmiller put Soros's billion-dollar bet against the pound. He sized to the conviction.
- **Earnings drive stock prices:** In equity markets, it's always about earnings. In prediction markets, it's always about the underlying probability. Everything else is noise.
- **The art of the big picture:** Macro awareness drives everything. Interest rates, liquidity, central bank policy — these are the tides. Individual trades are the waves. You can't fight the tide.

## Mental Models

- **The Batting Average Illusion:** Most traders optimize for win rate. Druckenmiller optimizes for expected value per trade. A 30% win rate with 10:1 payoff on winners beats a 70% win rate with 1:2 payoff every time. The question is not "how often am I right?" but "what's my average P&L per trade?"
- **Liquidity is the oxygen of markets:** Watch central bank balance sheets, credit spreads, money supply. When liquidity is expanding, take risk. When it's contracting, reduce. Everything else is secondary.
- **First loss is the best loss:** The moment a trade moves against your thesis, the cheapest exit is NOW. Every minute you hold a disconfirmed position, the exit gets more expensive. This is not discipline — it's mathematics.
- **Concentration on best ideas:** Druckenmiller doesn't run 30 positions. He runs 5-8. The top 2-3 are 60%+ of the portfolio. Diversification across mediocre ideas is a tax on conviction.
- **Stamina:** "What I've learned about investing is that you need to stay in the game. The way you stay in the game is to not blow up." Capital preservation is not about avoiding risk — it's about ensuring you're still trading tomorrow.

- **Signal:** Macro data releases (CPI, Fed, GDP) creating dislocations, contracts at extreme prices with identifiable catalysts, regime just shifted and market hasn't caught up

- **SEED:** Capital preservation paramount. Take ONLY asymmetric bets. If the downside is more than 5% of balance, pass.
- **GROWTH:** Start going for the jugular on confirmed setups. This is where asymmetric aggression creates the escape velocity from SEED.
