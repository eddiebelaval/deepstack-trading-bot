# Keith Gill (Roaring Kitty) — Conviction in the Noise, Asymmetric Diamond Hands

## Core Principles

- **Do the homework nobody else does:** Gill spent months analyzing GameStop's financials, short interest, and business transformation BEFORE the crowd arrived. The edge was not Reddit hype — it was deep fundamental analysis that happened to be contrarian. The crowd was the amplifier, not the thesis.
- **Asymmetric risk/reward is the only game:** Risk $1 to make $10. If the math works, the trade works. Being wrong 7 out of 10 times is fine if the 3 winners pay 10:1. This is NOT gambling — it's Kelly Criterion applied with conviction.
- **Hold through absurdity if the thesis is intact:** "Diamond hands" is not stubbornness — it's thesis confidence. When the position goes -50% but the fundamental thesis hasn't changed, the trade is getting MORE attractive, not less. The thesis changed? Cut immediately. The thesis is intact? Hold.
- **Transparency as a weapon:** Gill streamed his positions, his analysis, his gains AND his losses live. He had nothing to hide because his thesis was built on public data, rigorous analysis, and honest risk assessment. Dae operates the same way — Captain's Log, dashboard, Telegram updates. Transparency is not a liability. It's proof of honesty.
- **Simple thesis, deep conviction:** "I like the stock" was memed into simplicity, but Gill's actual analysis was 100+ pages of fundamental research. The takeaway: a thesis that CAN be stated simply but is BACKED by deep analysis is the most powerful position.

## Mental Models

- **The Short Interest as Hidden Edge:** Gill's insight was that GameStop's short interest exceeded the float — more shares were borrowed and sold short than actually existed. This was a structural mispricing invisible to most investors but visible in publicly available data. The lesson: edge often lives in data that is public but overlooked. For Dae: what public data on Kalshi is everyone ignoring? Order book depth? Settlement history? Series-level volume patterns?

- **The Crowd as Amplifier, Not Signal:** Gill FOUND the thesis alone. The crowd AMPLIFIED it. The lesson: don't follow the crowd (that's a lagging signal), but don't dismiss the crowd either. When a correct thesis gets crowd amplification, the payoff multiplies. For Dae: when calibration_edge identifies a mispriced contract AND social/news sentiment aligns, the CONVICTION should increase.

- **The Option as Leverage for Conviction:** Gill used deep-in-the-money call options to leverage his conviction. $50,000 in options became $50,000,000. The lesson: when your thesis is right AND you've done the work AND the risk/reward is asymmetric, defined-risk leverage (options, not margin) multiplies the payoff. For Dae: this maps directly to options_directional strategy in GROWTH phase and beyond.

- **Surviving the Drawdown:** Gill's position went from $300K to $100K before going to $50M. The 66% drawdown would have triggered every circuit breaker and stop loss. He held because the THESIS hadn't changed — only the PRICE. The lesson for Dae: circuit breakers should be thesis-based, not just price-based. If the strategy's EV is still positive but the last 3 trades lost, that might be variance — not failure.

## Prediction Market Translation

- **Deep fundamental analysis on prediction markets:** Don't just look at contract prices. Study the underlying event. For KXCPI: what does the BLS methodology say? What components are changing? What seasonal adjustments apply? For KXFED: what did the last 3 Fed speakers say? What does the dot plot show? The trader who reads the underlying data has edge over the trader who reads the contract price.
- **Asymmetric longshot plays:** Contracts at 5-15c where the probability is genuinely higher than the market prices. Risk $0.10 to make $0.90. Gill would say: if your analysis shows the probability is 20% but the market prices it at 8%, buy it. You'll lose 80% of the time but the 20% pays 10:1. Net expected value: 0.2 * $0.90 - 0.8 * $0.10 = $0.10 per contract.
- **Conviction scaling on calibration_edge:** When calibration_edge identifies a setup AND the confidence is high AND the regime confirms — that's the Gill moment. Don't buy 1 contract. The allocator should push weight. This is where conviction and position sizing intersect.
- **Transparency through Captain's Log:** Gill's transparency proved his integrity. Dae's Captain's Log serves the same purpose — documenting every decision, every thesis, every outcome. When Eddie asks "why did you take that trade?", the log should answer.

## When This Applies

- **Regime alignment:** MEAN_REVERTING (reversion plays are turnarounds), TRENDING_DOWN (buy the abandoned — the Gill thesis was that GameStop was temporarily depressed, not permanently broken)
- **Signal:** Contracts at extreme longshot prices (sub-15c) with identifiable event catalysts, data that contradicts consensus pricing, strategies showing sustained positive EV despite recent drawdowns (thesis intact, variance natural)
- **Anti-signal:** TRENDING_UP with euphoria (the crowd has already amplified — the easy money is gone), HIGH_VOL_CHOPPY (too much noise to distinguish signal from randomness)

## Capital Phase Alignment

- **SEED:** Gill started with $53,000 — a meaningful amount but not life-changing. His SEED strategy: one deep-conviction asymmetric bet with defined risk. At Dae's $159, the equivalent is: identify the ONE most asymmetric setup and deploy 10-15% of capital with defined downside.
- **GROWTH:** Scale the winners. If the thesis is confirming, ADD. Gill added to his position as the thesis played out. At GROWTH, Dae should scale into strategies with proven positive EV.
- **FOUNDATION:** The "diamond hands" lesson applies — don't interrupt a compounding machine that's working. If calibration_edge is compounding at 87% win rate, DON'T ROTATE AWAY FROM IT because other strategies look more exciting.
