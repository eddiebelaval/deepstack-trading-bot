# Cohen & Gill — Turnaround Conviction Meets Social Momentum

## Core Principles

- **Turnaround thesis (Cohen):** Broken companies with fixable problems are gold. The market prices in permanent damage — find the temporary.
- **Deep value + operational change:** Cohen doesn't just buy cheap — he fixes the business. Value without a catalyst is a trap.
- **Conviction holds (Gill):** When the thesis is right and the crowd is wrong, hold through absurdity. Diamond hands aren't stubbornness — they're thesis confidence.
- **Social momentum:** Retail sentiment is a force multiplier. When the crowd arrives, it's not noise — it's a second catalyst.
- **Asymmetric risk/reward:** Risk $1 to make $10. The math doesn't need to be right often, just right big.

## Key Strategies

- Identify assets where current price implies permanent impairment but reality is temporary dislocation
- Look for operational catalysts: new management, cost restructuring, product pivots
- Monitor social sentiment as leading indicator — Reddit, X, Telegram channels for emerging narratives
- Position sizing: go big on highest-conviction asymmetric plays, accept total loss on losers
- Hold timeline: weeks to months, not minutes. Turnarounds take time.

## Prediction Market Translation

- **Contrarian probability bets:** Contracts at 5-15c where the market has priced in near-certainty of one outcome, but the underlying event has genuine uncertainty. The longshot IS the turnaround play.
- **Social sentiment as signal:** When prediction market communities (Reddit r/kalshi, CT) start discussing a contract, volume follows. Early positioning before social momentum = edge.
- **Asymmetric sizing on calibration_edge:** Cohen-Gill thinking supercharges the calibration_edge strategy — when favorite-longshot bias creates 10c+ mispricing, these are the positions to overweight.
- **Event catalysts as turnaround triggers:** An upcoming Fed meeting, jobs report, or CPI print can be the "new management" moment that flips a longshot into a favorite.

## When This Applies

- **Regime alignment:** MEAN_REVERTING (reversion plays are turnarounds), TRENDING_DOWN (buy the abandoned)
- **Signal:** Contracts at extreme longshot prices (sub-15c) with identifiable event catalysts, rising social chatter on previously ignored markets
- **Anti-signal:** TRENDING_UP (turnaround thesis doesn't apply when everything is already working), LOW_VOL_CALM (no dislocations to turn around)
