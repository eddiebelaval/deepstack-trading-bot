# Musk & Jobs — First Principles, Market Creation

## Core Principles

- **First principles thinking (Musk):** Don't reason by analogy. Break problems down to fundamental truths and rebuild from there.
- **Skate to where the puck is going (Jobs):** The market tells you where it's been. Vision tells you where it's going.
- **Create the market, don't follow it:** The biggest wins come from seeing a market that doesn't exist yet and building for it.
- **Vertical integration:** Control the entire stack. Dependencies on others are failure points.
- **Simplicity is the ultimate sophistication (Jobs):** Complex systems fail. Simple systems that do one thing exceptionally well compound.
- **High pain tolerance:** Both Musk and Jobs built through periods where reasonable people would quit. Conviction survives setbacks.

## Key Strategies

- Identify emerging categories before the crowd (new Kalshi market series, new event types)
- Be first mover in new prediction market categories — liquidity providers earn spread
- Build systematic advantage: automate what others do manually
- Vertical integration: own data collection, analysis, execution, and learning in one system
- Iterate rapidly: ship, measure, adjust. Perfection is the enemy of edge.

## Prediction Market Translation

- **New market first-mover advantage:** When Kalshi launches new event categories, the early contracts are often mispriced because historical calibration data doesn't exist. First-principles analysis of the underlying event (not historical contract prices) creates edge.
- **Build the system, not the trade:** Musk/Jobs thinking applied to trading means building Dae itself — a system that gets better over time through automated learning, not a series of individual trades.
- **Weather/climate markets as first principles:** Weather prediction is pure physics. No social sentiment, no management teams, no earnings surprises. First-principles analysis of weather models vs. market probabilities = structural edge.
- **Simplicity in strategy:** Don't overcomplicate. calibration_edge works because the thesis is simple: markets misprice probabilities at the tails. One strategy executed well beats seven mediocre strategies.

## When This Applies

- **Regime alignment:** All regimes (systems thinking is regime-agnostic), especially valuable during REGIME TRANSITIONS when old models break
- **Signal:** New Kalshi series launches, market structure changes, new data sources becoming available, regime transitions where historical patterns break down
- **Anti-signal:** Mature, well-arbitraged markets with deep liquidity (the crowd has already done the first-principles analysis)
