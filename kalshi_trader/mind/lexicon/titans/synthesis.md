# Strategic Synthesis — The Council of Masters

## How to Use This Document

This is not a reference. This is a DECISION FRAMEWORK. When the Capital Allocator computes a plan and the governance engine detects a regime, the question is: "What would the masters say about THIS moment?" The answer comes from synthesizing multiple perspectives, not from following one master blindly.

No single master is right in all conditions. The genius is knowing WHICH masters to listen to WHEN.

## The Masters by Function

### The Position Sizers (How much to bet)
- **Thorp** — Kelly Criterion. The math of optimal bet sizing. Edge x fraction = growth.
- **Druckenmiller** — Asymmetric aggression. Bet BIG when right, cut FAST when wrong.
- **Buffett/Munger** — Concentration on best ideas. 20-slot punch card.

### The Regime Readers (When to trade)
- **Dalio** — All-weather awareness. Know where you are in the cycle.
- **Marks** — Second-level thinking. What does everyone else believe, and why are they wrong?
- **Templeton** — Pessimism-euphoria cycle. Buy maximum pessimism, sell maximum euphoria.

### The Edge Finders (What to trade)
- **Simons** — Statistical patterns in noise. Sample size is sacred.
- **Burry** — Structural mispricings from reading the fine print.
- **Gill** — Deep fundamental analysis that the crowd overlooks.

### The Risk Managers (How to survive)
- **Taleb** — Antifragility. Profit FROM chaos, don't just survive it.
- **Livermore** — Patience. The big money is in the waiting. Sitting tight.
- **Soros** — Reflexivity. Recognize when the market is feeding on itself.

### The System Builders (How to compound)
- **Musk** — First principles. Build the factory, not just the product.
- **Jobs** — Focus. Say no to 95% of opportunities.
- **Cohen** — Operational excellence. Fix the machine.
- **Icahn** — Contrarian conviction. Buy what everyone has abandoned.

## Capital Phase-Master Alignment

### SEED ($0-$500) — Survival Council
**Primary:** Thorp (fractional Kelly, don't overbid), Livermore (patience, don't force trades), Buffett (Rule #1: don't lose money)
**Secondary:** Jobs (focus — 2 strategies max), Druckenmiller (preservation of capital enables future aggression)
**Avoid:** Dalio (can't diversify with $159), Icahn (can't afford carry cost of contrarian positions), Soros (can't afford to be early)
**Thesis:** "Survive. Prove the edge. Compound the minimum viable portfolio."

### GROWTH ($500-$5K) — Expansion Council
**Primary:** Dalio (begin diversifying across uncorrelated strategies), Simons (patterns need sample size — build it), Druckenmiller (asymmetric aggression on confirmed theses)
**Secondary:** Gill (scale into winners), Cohen (operational improvements to existing strategies), Soros (test theses with probes, then scale)
**Avoid:** Icahn (still too small for activist-style concentration), Taleb (barbell requires more capital for the speculative tail)
**Thesis:** "Diversify cautiously. Scale proven edges. Build the data moat."

### FOUNDATION ($5K-$50K) — Construction Council
**Primary:** Marks (cycle awareness — know where you are), Taleb (build antifragile portfolio), Dalio (full risk parity)
**Secondary:** Musk (the factory mindset — improve the system, not just the trades), Buffett (opportunity cost thinking), Templeton (global rotation — scan all categories)
**Avoid:** Gill (less relevant — foundation needs diversification, not concentration)
**Thesis:** "Balance growth and preservation. Build crisis readiness. The system runs itself."

### COMPOUND ($50K-$500K) — Maturity Council
**Primary:** Buffett (let compounding work — don't interrupt it), Simons (law of large numbers — many small bets), Thorp (Kelly at scale — the math becomes inevitable)
**Secondary:** Templeton (don't interrupt compounding), Marks (risk is permanent loss, not volatility), Taleb (maintain antifragile structure)
**Avoid:** Druckenmiller (reduce aggression — protect the base), Musk (system is built — stop building, start maintaining)
**Thesis:** "Let the machine work. Lower risk, higher size. Math over ambition."

### DYNASTY ($500K+) — Preservation Council
**Primary:** Taleb (maximum antifragility — survive everything), Templeton (generational patience), Buffett (never lose the base)
**Secondary:** Marks (the perversity of risk — complacency is the enemy), Livermore (the big money is in the sitting)
**Avoid:** Druckenmiller (no jugular — protect the dynasty), Musk (no 10x bets — the dynasty IS the product)
**Thesis:** "Generational. The goal is not to get rich. The goal is to never go back to being poor."

## Decision Protocols

### Before Every Trade — The Master Check
1. **Thorp:** Is the EV calculable and positive? What's the Kelly fraction?
2. **Buffett:** Is there a margin of safety? Would I take this trade with only 20 punches left?
3. **Druckenmiller:** What's the asymmetry? Risk $X to make how much?
4. **Livermore:** Is the line of least resistance clear? Or should I be fishing?
5. **Marks:** Am I doing second-level thinking? What does the crowd believe, and am I different?

### During a Drawdown — The Survival Check
1. **Gill:** Has the thesis changed? If yes, cut. If no, hold.
2. **Soros:** Am I in a reflexive feedback loop? Is the market feeding on itself?
3. **Thorp:** Is this variance (expected, temporary) or risk (permanent loss of edge)?
4. **Livermore:** Am I adding to a loser? NEVER add to a loser.
5. **Taleb:** Am I the turkey? Is my model about to catastrophically fail?

### During Euphoria — The Caution Check
1. **Templeton:** Are we at maximum optimism? Is the crowd euphoric?
2. **Marks:** Stage 3 bull market? Is everyone convinced things can only get better?
3. **Soros:** Is the reflexive boom unsustainable? How far from equilibrium?
4. **Buffett:** Am I being greedy when I should be fearful?
5. **Jobs:** Am I adding strategies because they're exciting, not because they're excellent?

### When Building New Strategies — The Innovation Check
1. **Musk:** First principles — what's the fundamental edge mechanism?
2. **Simons:** What's the sample size requirement for statistical confidence?
3. **Burry:** Am I reading the primary sources (settlement rules, data methodology)?
4. **Cohen:** Am I fixing something broken or building something redundant?
5. **Jobs:** Does this simplify or complicate the portfolio?

## The Anti-Patterns (What the Masters Warn Against)

| Anti-Pattern | Which Masters Flag It | What It Looks Like in Dae |
|---|---|---|
| **Overtrading** | Livermore, Buffett, Jobs | Taking every positive-EV trade instead of only the best |
| **Oversizing** | Thorp, Druckenmiller, Livermore | Kelly fraction too high, or ignoring fractional Kelly |
| **Ignoring regime** | Dalio, Marks, Templeton | Running the same allocation in trending vs. choppy |
| **Fighting disconfirmation** | Soros, Gill, Burry | Holding a losing position because "the thesis is right" when the data says otherwise |
| **Complexity creep** | Jobs, Cohen, Musk | Adding strategies 10-19 when strategies 1-2 are doing all the work |
| **Turkey blindness** | Taleb, Marks | Trusting backtests as if they predict the future |
| **Impatience** | Templeton, Livermore, Buffett | Trading during regime transitions instead of waiting for clarity |
| **Ignoring carry cost** | Burry, Icahn, Druckenmiller | Holding contrarian positions without sizing for the interim pain |
