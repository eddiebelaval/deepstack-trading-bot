# George Soros — Reflexivity, The Feedback Loop Hunter

## Core Principles

- **Reflexivity:** Markets are not passive reflectors of reality — they actively change the reality they're supposed to reflect. Prices influence fundamentals, which influence prices. This feedback loop is where the biggest opportunities and the biggest dangers live.
- **Bet big when you're right:** The size of your position when you're right matters more than your win rate. A 40% win rate with 5:1 payoff ratio destroys a 60% win rate with 1:1.
- **Test your thesis with a position:** Start small. If the market confirms, add aggressively. If it doesn't, cut immediately. The market is the ultimate laboratory.
- **Boom-bust model:** Every trend carries the seeds of its own reversal. Identify where in the boom-bust cycle you are. The trend feeds on itself (reflexivity) until the gap between perception and reality becomes unsustainable.
- **Fallibility:** You will be wrong. The question is how quickly you recognize it and how little damage you take. Soros's genius was not in being right — it was in cutting losses at the first sign of disconfirmation.

## Mental Models

- **The Reflexive Feedback Loop:**
  1. A prevailing bias emerges (consensus narrative)
  2. The bias influences market prices
  3. Market prices influence real-world outcomes (self-fulfilling prophecy)
  4. Real-world outcomes seem to confirm the bias
  5. The bias becomes more extreme
  6. Gap between perception and reality widens
  7. Eventually, reality asserts itself — the boom becomes the bust

- **Far-from-equilibrium conditions:** When reflexive feedback loops push markets far from rational pricing, the eventual correction is violent. These are the biggest opportunities — but they require timing, conviction, and the stomach to hold through pain.

- **The human uncertainty principle:** Markets are inherently uncertain because the observer (trader) is also a participant who changes the system being observed. This means prediction markets have a meta-layer: the prediction itself influences the outcome.

- **Thesis testing:** Every trade is a hypothesis. Treat it as such. If the evidence confirms, double down. If the evidence contradicts, kill it. Don't negotiate with disconfirmation.

## Prediction Market Translation

- **Self-fulfilling prediction markets:** Political prediction markets are PEAK REFLEXIVITY. When Kalshi shows a candidate at 70%, media reports "prediction markets show..." which influences voter behavior which influences the actual probability. The price IS partially the outcome. Soros would say: trade the reflexive loop, not the underlying event.
- **Boom-bust in contract pricing:** When a Kalshi contract trends from 30c to 70c on narrative momentum (not new data), that IS a reflexive boom. The question: is the gap between narrative and reality widening or narrowing? If widening, ride it. If narrowing, prepare for the bust.
- **Testing thesis with a probe position:** Buy 1 contract as a probe. If it moves in your direction with confirming data, add 3 more. If it moves against you immediately, you got your answer for 1 contract. The loss is the lesson, not the failure.
- **Political and macro reflexivity:** KXFED contracts are reflexive — Fed policy expectations (priced by Kalshi) influence bond markets, which influence economic conditions, which influence Fed policy. Trade the loop, not the endpoint.
- **Fallibility as a feature:** Dae should embrace being wrong quickly. The circuit breaker system IS Soros's principle encoded: recognize disconfirmation, cut, and redeploy. Speed of recognition > accuracy of prediction.

## When This Applies

- **Regime alignment:** TRENDING_UP/DOWN (reflexive feedback loops create trends), HIGH_VOL_CHOPPY (far-from-equilibrium conditions — boom-bust transitions)
- **Signal:** Price moving without new fundamental data (reflexive momentum), narrative diverging from measurable reality, extreme consensus forming on one side of a market
- **Anti-signal:** LOW_VOL_CALM (no reflexive feedback loops active — the boring market is the honest market), MEAN_REVERTING (reflexivity is about trends extending, not reverting)

## Capital Phase Alignment

- **SEED:** Test with probes. Every trade is a hypothesis. Cut fast. Soros's principle protects you — never risk the base on unconfirmed thesis.
- **GROWTH:** Start scaling into confirmed theses. This is where Soros's asymmetric sizing creates the compounding engine.
- **COMPOUND:** The boom-bust awareness protects accumulated capital. When you see reflexive extremes, reduce exposure — the correction is coming.
- **DYNASTY:** Reflexivity awareness becomes the guardian. The biggest threat to generational wealth is a reflexive feedback loop that you're on the wrong side of.
