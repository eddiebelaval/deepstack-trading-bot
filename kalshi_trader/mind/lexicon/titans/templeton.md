# John Templeton — The Cycle Prophet, Maximum Pessimism

## Core Principles

- **Maximum pessimism:** "Bull markets are born on pessimism, grow on skepticism, mature on optimism, and die on euphoria." Buy at maximum pessimism. Sell at maximum euphoria. This is the single most powerful market timing framework in existence.
- **Global perspective:** While others trade one market, Templeton scanned the entire world. When US markets were euphoric, Japan was in pessimism. When Japan boomed, emerging markets were depressed. There is ALWAYS a market in pessimism somewhere.
- **Patience measured in years, not hours:** Templeton's average holding period was 4-5 years. He bought when others were selling, then waited — sometimes for years — for the cycle to turn. The edge was not in the entry. It was in the willingness to wait.
- **Diversification across geography and time:** True diversification is not 10 stocks in the S&P 500. It's positions in uncorrelated markets at different points in their cycles. When one is in euphoria (sell), another is in pessimism (buy).
- **Humility before the market:** "The four most dangerous words in investing are: 'this time it's different.'" History rhymes. Cycles repeat. The investor who thinks THIS time the cycle won't complete is betting against all of history.

## Mental Models

- **The Pessimism-Euphoria Cycle:**
  1. **Maximum Pessimism** — Everyone has sold. Volume is dead. Headlines are catastrophic. THIS is the buy zone.
  2. **Skepticism** — Early recovery. The crowd says "dead cat bounce." Prices improve but confidence is low. Hold and add.
  3. **Optimism** — The crowd starts buying. Prices accelerate. "The bottom is in." Start reducing position size.
  4. **Euphoria** — "It can only go up." New participants flood in. SELL. Maximum euphoria = maximum risk.

- **Global cycle rotation:** Templeton's insight: the cycle plays out in different markets at different times. When US prediction markets are in euphoria (high consensus, high volume, tight spreads), look at undertraded categories. When crypto contracts are in pessimism (crashed prices, volume dried up), that's the entry.

- **The bargain hunter's checklist:** Templeton had criteria for maximum pessimism opportunities:
  1. Price below intrinsic value (contract below true probability)
  2. Universal hatred (low volume, negative sentiment)
  3. No institutional interest (ignored by sophisticated traders)
  4. Temporary cause (not permanent structural change)
  5. Catalyst for reversion (upcoming event, data release, seasonal pattern)

- **Compound interest as the 8th wonder:** Templeton's real edge was time. A 12% annual return for 50 years turns $10,000 into $2.9 million. The implication: DON'T INTERRUPT COMPOUNDING. Every unnecessary loss, every forced sale, every emotional exit interrupts the compounding machine.

## When This Applies

- **Regime alignment:** TRENDING_DOWN (maximum pessimism territory — the buy zone), LOW_VOL_CALM (patience rewarded — wait for the setup), regime TRANSITIONS from pessimism to skepticism (the early recovery phase)
- **Signal:** Low volume in a previously active Kalshi series (crowd has left), contracts at extreme prices with upcoming catalysts (the bargain), governance detecting regime shift from choppy to calm (pessimism transitioning to skepticism)
- **Anti-signal:** HIGH_VOL_CHOPPY (too early — pessimism hasn't bottomed yet), TRENDING_UP with euphoria indicators (volume spike, consensus, new participants — this is the sell zone)
