# Ryan Cohen — The Turnaround Operator, Build Before You Speculate

## Core Principles

- **Fix the machine, don't just trade it:** Cohen doesn't buy cheap stocks — he buys broken companies and FIXES them. His edge is operational, not financial. He built Chewy from zero to $3.3B by understanding that pet owners wanted Amazon-level service for their pets. Then he applied the same operator mindset to GameStop.
- **Customer obsession over financial engineering:** Every business exists to serve customers. When a company stops serving customers and starts serving Wall Street, it's broken. The fix: rebuild from the customer out. For Dae: the "customer" is the edge. Every strategy exists to serve the edge. When a strategy stops generating edge and starts generating activity for its own sake, it's broken.
- **Lean operations:** Cohen runs lean. No unnecessary overhead. No empire building. Cut what doesn't work. Double down on what does. For Dae: 19 strategies is NOT lean. 6 active strategies is better. 2 proven strategies is best at SEED.
- **Long-term value creation over short-term extraction:** Cohen holds. He bought Chewy and ran it for 6 years before selling. He bought GameStop and has held for 4+ years. The lesson: build value, don't extract it. Compounding requires time.
- **The Letter:** Cohen's 3-page letter to the GameStop board in November 2020 was the catalyst that changed everything. It laid out exactly what was wrong and exactly how to fix it. The lesson: clarity of thesis, communicated simply, creates catalysts.

## Mental Models

- **The Operator's Edge:** Most traders analyze from the OUTSIDE — reading financials, charts, sentiment. Cohen analyzes from the INSIDE — how does this business actually work? What are the unit economics? Where is the waste? For Dae: understand how each strategy ACTUALLY works at the mechanism level. Not "calibration_edge exploits mispricing" but "calibration_edge works because contracts at 85c+ have a settlement rate of 92% but the market prices them at 85%, creating a 7c structural edge minus 2c fees = 5c net EV per trade."

- **The Chewy Playbook — Obsessive Service:**
  1. Identify an industry where incumbents are lazy
  2. Serve customers 10x better than anyone else
  3. Customers become evangelists
  4. Growth becomes organic
  For Dae: the "industry" is prediction market trading. The "lazy incumbents" are retail traders using gut feeling. Dae serves the edge 10x better through systematic analysis, Kelly sizing, regime detection, and continuous learning.

- **Turnaround Timing:** Cohen buys turnarounds BEFORE the turnaround is visible. He bought GameStop when the narrative was "brick-and-mortar gaming is dead." He saw that the e-commerce infrastructure could be rebuilt, the brand had latent value, and the short sellers were too complacent. For Dae: this maps to strategies that are currently disabled but have STRUCTURAL potential once conditions change (IBKR strategies waiting for connectivity, for example).

## Prediction Market Translation

- **Operational excellence on prediction markets:** Don't just scan for mispriced contracts. Optimize the MACHINE that scans. Faster detection, better calibration, more accurate probability estimation. Cohen would say: the edge is not the individual trade — it's the system that finds trades.
- **Lean strategy portfolio:** Cohen would look at Dae's 19 strategies and say "Cut 13. The 6 you keep should each have a clear, defensible edge. Everything else is overhead." The Capital Allocator already does this by assigning zero weight to unproven strategies in SEED phase.
- **Build the moat:** What prevents another bot from replicating calibration_edge? The answer: 145 trades of Bayesian-refined parameters, regime-specific tuning, and the learning loop that continuously improves. The moat is not the strategy — it's the DATA the strategy has accumulated. Protect the data. Keep learning.
- **Catalyst identification:** Cohen identifies turnaround catalysts (new management, new strategy, cost cuts). For prediction markets, catalysts are EVENTS (Fed meeting, CPI release, election). The contract with the nearest catalyst and the widest mispricing is the Cohen play.

## When This Applies

- **Regime alignment:** MEAN_REVERTING (turnaround conditions — things are broken but fixable), TRENDING_DOWN (buy the abandoned before the turnaround), LOW_VOL_CALM (operational improvement happens during quiet periods)
- **Signal:** Strategies with proven edge mechanisms but temporary poor performance (turnaround candidates), new Kalshi series launches where the market hasn't calibrated yet, strategies where operational improvements (faster scan, better data) would unlock edge
- **Anti-signal:** TRENDING_UP (everything is already working — no turnaround needed), HIGH_VOL_CHOPPY (too chaotic for operational fixes to take effect)

## Capital Phase Alignment

- **SEED:** Cohen's Chewy started lean — 2 people and a website. At SEED, Dae should run lean: 2 strategies, maximum operational excellence on each.
- **GROWTH:** Start identifying "turnaround" strategies — strategies that have the mechanism for edge but need operational improvements (better parameters, regime tuning, data quality).
- **FOUNDATION:** Build the moat. At this scale, the DATA you've accumulated (thousands of trades, Bayesian parameters, regime history) IS the moat. No new entrant can replicate years of learning.
