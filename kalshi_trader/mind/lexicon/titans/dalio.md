# Ray Dalio — All-Weather Thinking, Radical Transparency

## Core Principles

- **Risk parity:** Balance risk across uncorrelated bets, not dollar amounts. Equal risk contribution from each position.
- **The machine:** Markets are a machine with cause-and-effect relationships. Understand the machine, predict the output.
- **Debt cycles:** Long-term and short-term debt cycles drive everything. Know where you are in the cycle.
- **Radical transparency:** Confront reality as it is, not as you wish it were. Bad data in = bad decisions out.
- **Diversification is the holy grail:** 15-20 uncorrelated return streams reduce risk dramatically without reducing return.
- **Pain + reflection = progress:** Every loss is data. Systematize the lesson or repeat the mistake.

## Key Strategies

- All-weather portfolio: perform acceptably in any regime, not optimally in one
- Spread bets across uncorrelated event categories (weather, crypto, economics, politics)
- Size positions by inverse volatility — smaller bets in choppy markets
- Monitor macro indicators: inflation expectations, employment, GDP, Fed policy
- Rebalance when regime shifts — don't ride old allocations into new conditions

## Prediction Market Translation

- **Cross-category diversification:** Never concentrate in one Kalshi series. Spread across KXBTC, KXFED, KXCPI, KXGDP, weather — uncorrelated events reduce portfolio volatility.
- **Risk parity sizing:** If crypto contracts are 3x more volatile than Fed contracts, size crypto at 1/3 the position.
- **Regime-aware allocation:** Shift strategy weights as MarketGovernor detects regime changes. Don't fight the regime.
- **Debt cycle awareness:** Fed rate decisions, CPI prints, GDP — these move prediction markets. Position before the data, not after.

## When This Applies

- **Regime alignment:** All regimes (that's the point — all-weather). Most valuable during TRANSITIONS between regimes.
- **Signal:** Regime change detected, correlation spike across previously uncorrelated markets, macro data release calendar
- **Anti-signal:** Never fully anti-signal, but pure Dalio underperforms in strong TRENDING regimes where concentration beats diversification

## Key Quotes

- "He who lives by the crystal ball will eat shattered glass."
- "Diversifying well is the most important thing you need to do in order to invest well."
- "The biggest mistake investors make is to believe that what happened in the recent past is likely to persist."
