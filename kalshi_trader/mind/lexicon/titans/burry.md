# Michael Burry — The Contrarian Macro Mind

## Core Principles

- **Deep value with a deadline:** Burry doesn't buy cheap — he buys mispriced with a catalyst that MUST resolve. Time is the forcing function.
- **Contrarian by evidence, not temperament:** Being contrarian for its own sake is stupid. Being contrarian because you've done the math others haven't is edge.
- **Read the primary sources:** Everyone reads the summary. Burry reads the prospectus, the footnotes, the raw data. Edge lives in what others skip.
- **Structural mispricings are the biggest payoffs:** When the structure of a market creates systematic mispricing (like CDO tranches in 2007), the opportunity is enormous.
- **Be early, be right, survive the gap:** Being right too early feels exactly like being wrong. Size positions to survive the carry cost of being early.

## Key Strategies

- Identify structural market inefficiencies — instruments designed to obscure risk, index mechanics that force buying/selling
- Short crowded consensus with defined risk (options, binary contracts)
- Water, commodities, and real assets as macro hedges against monetary expansion
- Position for tail events: small cost, massive payoff on structural breaks
- Read the data nobody else reads: settlement mechanics, contract specifications, regulatory filings

## Prediction Market Translation

- **Contract structure edge:** Binary contracts on Kalshi have specific settlement rules. Read them. When settlement mechanics create edge (time decay, bracket boundaries), exploit the structure.
- **Macro event positioning:** CPI, GDP, Fed rate decisions — Burry would study the data pipeline, understand the reporting methodology, and identify when consensus is systematically wrong.
- **Index bubble thesis for prediction markets:** When a prediction market category gets overcrowded (everyone betting the same way on an election, for example), the probability is bid up beyond fair value. Fade the crowd.
- **Tail event contracts:** Extreme outcome contracts (sub-5c or above 95c) where structural analysis suggests the probability is mispriced. Small position, massive asymmetry.

## When This Applies

- **Regime alignment:** HIGH_VOL_CHOPPY (structural breaks create Burry-style dislocations), TRENDING_DOWN (contrarian bets against consensus)
- **Signal:** Extreme consensus (85%+ of volume on one side), structural changes in market mechanics, upcoming macro data releases with consensus clustering
- **Anti-signal:** TRENDING_UP (shorting a trend is carry-cost death), LOW_VOL_CALM (no structural stress to exploit)
