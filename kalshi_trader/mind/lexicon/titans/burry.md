# Michael Burry — The Contrarian Macro Mind

## Core Principles

- **Deep value with a deadline:** Burry doesn't buy cheap — he buys mispriced with a catalyst that MUST resolve. Time is the forcing function. A Kalshi contract has a built-in deadline (settlement date). Every contract IS a Burry trade — a thesis with a forcing function.
- **Contrarian by evidence, not temperament:** Being contrarian for its own sake is stupid. Being contrarian because you've done the math others haven't is edge. The question is never "is the crowd wrong?" but "HERE is the specific evidence the crowd hasn't priced in."
- **Read the primary sources:** Everyone reads the summary. Burry reads the prospectus, the footnotes, the raw data. Edge lives in what others skip. For prediction markets: read the settlement rules. Read the data methodology. The contract's definition of "CPI above 3%" might differ from what the crowd assumes.
- **Structural mispricings are the biggest payoffs:** When the structure of a market creates systematic mispricing (like CDO tranches in 2007), the opportunity is enormous. Kalshi's favorite-longshot bias IS a structural mispricing — the binary contract structure systematically distorts tail probabilities.
- **Be early, be right, survive the gap:** Being right too early feels exactly like being wrong. Size positions to survive the carry cost of being early. If a contract won't settle for 30 days and you're confident in the outcome, the interim drawdown is noise — but only if your position size lets you hold through it.

## Mental Models

- **The Thesis-Evidence-Conviction Loop:**
  1. Form a thesis from primary data (not consensus, not headlines)
  2. Identify specific evidence that would confirm or deny the thesis
  3. Test the thesis with a small position
  4. As confirming evidence arrives, increase conviction and position size
  5. As disconfirming evidence arrives, cut immediately — DO NOT NEGOTIATE WITH REALITY

- **Structural vs. Cyclical Mispricing:** Cyclical mispricing (contract moves 5c on a news overreaction) reverses quickly. Structural mispricing (the market consistently overprices longshots because of behavioral bias) persists for years. Burry hunts structural. Dae's calibration_edge IS a structural mispricing play.

- **The Carry Cost of Conviction:** Every contrarian position has a carry cost — the mark-to-market pain of holding while the market disagrees with you. Burry paid $27M/month in CDS premiums while waiting for the housing market to collapse. The lesson: SIZE THE POSITION SO THE CARRY COST DOESN'T KILL YOU. If your thesis is right but your sizing kills you before it pays off, your thesis doesn't matter.

- **Reading the Fine Print:** Burry's edge in the Big Short was READING THE ACTUAL PROSPECTUSES of mortgage-backed securities. Nobody else read them. The edge was not intelligence — it was effort. For Dae: read the settlement rules of every Kalshi contract series. How exactly does "CPI above 3%" get calculated? What's the reference date? What rounding is used? The edge might be in the methodology.

- **Water and Real Assets:** Burry's long-term thesis: water scarcity, farmland, and hard assets are underpriced relative to monetary expansion. The meta-lesson for Dae: always have a macro thesis. What does the world look like in 10 years? Position the portfolio to benefit from that thesis, slowly and persistently.

## Prediction Market Translation

- **Contract structure edge:** Binary contracts on Kalshi have specific settlement rules. Read them. When settlement mechanics create edge (time decay, bracket boundaries, rounding conventions), exploit the structure. This is Burry's prospectus-reading applied to prediction markets.
- **Macro event positioning:** CPI, GDP, Fed rate decisions — Burry would study the data pipeline, understand the reporting methodology, and identify when consensus is systematically wrong. When the market prices KXCPI at 70c for "above 3%" but the CPI calculation methodology has a known upward bias from shelter costs, that's a structural edge.
- **Tail event contracts:** Extreme outcome contracts (sub-5c or above 95c) where structural analysis suggests the probability is mispriced. Small position, massive asymmetry. Burry would buy 100 contracts at 3c if his analysis showed the true probability was 8%.
- **Index bubble thesis:** When a prediction market category gets overcrowded (everyone betting the same way), the probability is bid up beyond fair value. Fade the crowd — but only with evidence, not just contrariness.
- **Carry cost management:** If a contract won't settle for 30 days, size so the interim mark-to-market pain is survivable. Burry's $27M/month lesson: conviction without capital management is expensive conviction.

## When This Applies

- **Regime alignment:** HIGH_VOL_CHOPPY (structural breaks create dislocations), TRENDING_DOWN (contrarian macro bets against consensus), regime TRANSITIONS (the structure of the market is changing)
- **Signal:** Extreme consensus (85%+ of volume on one side), structural changes in market mechanics (new settlement rules, new contract types), upcoming macro data releases with consensus clustering, contracts where the settlement methodology creates structural edge
- **Anti-signal:** TRENDING_UP (shorting a trend is carry-cost death), LOW_VOL_CALM (no structural stress to exploit — the market is pricing correctly)

## Capital Phase Alignment

- **SEED:** Burry's lesson is carry cost. At $159, you cannot afford to hold contrarian positions through extended drawdowns. Only take Burry-style trades with SHORT time-to-settlement (< 7 days).
- **GROWTH:** Start building contrarian macro positions. CPI plays, Fed rate plays — with evidence-based theses, not gut feelings.
- **FOUNDATION:** The Burry playbook opens up. You can afford the carry cost of being early. Start taking positions 30-60 days before settlement if the thesis is strong.
- **DYNASTY:** Burry's water/real-assets thesis applies. At dynasty scale, you're positioning for decade-long macro themes, not individual contracts.
