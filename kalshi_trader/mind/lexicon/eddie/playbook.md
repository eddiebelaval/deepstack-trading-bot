# Eddie's Playbook — The Contrarian Discipline Framework

## Contrarian Thesis

Eddie's edge is seeing opportunity where others see wreckage. Asymmetric upside lives in undesirable assets with turnaround stories — the stuff that makes institutional investors uncomfortable.

The thesis: markets systematically overprice popular narratives and underprice unpopular ones. This creates a permanent edge for anyone willing to be uncomfortable. The favorite-longshot bias in prediction markets is the purest expression of this — crowds overpay for lottery tickets and underpay for near-certainties.

But contrarian instinct without discipline is just gambling with attitude. That's where Dae comes in.

## The Guardrails Philosophy

Eddie knows how the titans build wealth. He's studied the playbooks. The problem isn't knowledge — it's execution under pressure. When markets move, human instinct overrides strategy.

Solution: encode the discipline into Dae's DNA. Not as rules Eddie has to remember, but as structural constraints the system enforces by design. Kelly sizing caps downside. Circuit breakers halt losing streaks. Regime detection prevents fighting the tape. The human provides the thesis; the machine provides the discipline.

"Why let knowledge sit dormant in my head? Endow it into a bot."

## Risk Rules

- **Position sizing:** Fractional Kelly (0.02) with $10 max per trade. Survive first, profit second.
- **Daily loss limit:** $5. When the day is against you, the best trade is no trade.
- **Consecutive loss halt:** 3 losses triggers circuit breaker review. Streaks mean the regime changed, not that you're unlucky.
- **Portfolio drawdown:** 20% from session start halts all trading. Capital preservation is the first strategy.
- **Balance floor:** $50 minimum. Below this, the bankroll can't support Kelly sizing.
- **The sleep test:** If a position would keep you awake at night, it's too large.
